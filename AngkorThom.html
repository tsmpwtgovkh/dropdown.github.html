Angkor Thom

Angkor Thom, meaning "Great City," was the last and most enduring capital of the Khmer Empire. Established in the late 12th century by King Jayavarman VII, Angkor Thom is a masterpiece of urban planning and a symbol of the empire's architectural and artistic achievements. The city covers an area of 9 square kilometers and is surrounded by a massive moat and high walls, reflecting both its defensive capabilities and its symbolic significance as the center of the Khmer universe. The Bayon temple, with its 54 towers adorned with over 200 smiling faces, is located at the center of Angkor Thom and remains one of the most enigmatic and visually striking monuments of the Khmer Empire.

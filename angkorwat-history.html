History Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat was constructed during the reign of King Suryavarman II (1113-1150) and originally served as a Hindu temple dedicated to Vishnu. The design of Angkor Wat reflects a shift in Khmer architecture, incorporating elements from earlier Hindu temples and integrating innovative design features such as the expansive moat and central tower. After the fall of the Khmer Empire, the temple gradually transitioned to a Buddhist site. It remained relatively undisturbed due to its remote location, allowing it to be preserved through centuries of change and conflict.

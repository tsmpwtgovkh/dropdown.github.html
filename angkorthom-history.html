History Angkor Thom

Angkor Thom was constructed by King Jayavarman VII in the late 12th century as the capital of the Khmer Empire. The city was designed to reflect the cosmic world order and was enclosed by a massive wall and moat for protection. Angkor Thom was a major center of political and religious life in the empire, featuring several grand monuments and temples. The Bayon temple, with its distinctive smiling faces, was a focal point of the city and represented the king’s divine rule. After the fall of the Khmer Empire, Angkor Thom fell into decline but remained an important site for archaeological exploration.

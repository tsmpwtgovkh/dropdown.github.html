History Banteay Srey

Banteay Srey was constructed in the late 10th century during the reign of King Rajendravarman II. The temple is unique for its detailed and well-preserved carvings, which are considered some of the finest examples of Khmer art. Dedicated to Shiva, Banteay Srey reflects a shift in artistic style from larger, grandiose temples to more intricately detailed ones. Its construction with pink sandstone allowed for finer carving techniques and contributed to the temple's reputation for its beautiful and delicate artwork. The temple was rediscovered in the early 20th century and has since been restored, becoming a major attraction for visitors and scholars.

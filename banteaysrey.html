Banteay Srey

Banteay Srey, often referred to as the "Citadel of Women," is one of the most exquisite and well-preserved temples of the Khmer Empire. Located about 25 kilometers north of the main Angkor complex, Banteay Srey was built in the late 10th century and is dedicated to the Hindu god Shiva. The temple is renowned for its intricate carvings and the delicate beauty of its architecture, which stands out in contrast to the grand scale of other Angkorian temples. Constructed from pink sandstone, Banteay Srey is noted for its elaborate carvings, depicting scenes from Hindu mythology, which are considered to be among the finest examples of classical Khmer art.
